Personal website



Why I've done it

I bought my first digital camera in 2007. In 2009 I wanted to publish my photos online but I also wanted to learn HTML, because everyone talked about it but I knew I wouldn't learn it at class. A friend of mine already owned a domain and advised me how to start. I bought the domain and on the 7th of August of 2009 (2009/08/07) the first version of my personal website was online.

Since that day, with every semester's end can a new iteration on its look, technology and content. What meant to be a photo gallery became much more. As I'm writing this text my website now hosts information about my projects, talks and articles; synopsis of my books and free samples; advices and my experiences on college so other students are more aware of what awaits them and what are the do's and don'ts on college.


Lessons learned

As Adam Westbrook said to learn something you need to create a project that uses that knowledge. This website was (and still is) a project for me to learn HTML. Fortunately it lead me to more technologies - CSS, PHP, JavaScript, domains and hosting services - and more skills - WebDev techniques and tools, Website analytics and optimization, Mobile WebDev, SEO practices, Social networking/marketing. It was also a opportunity to discover and use frameworks like Twitter Bootstrap (a grid that turns into a list when the browser has a low resolution, e.g. mobile) and Masonry (a container that automatically resizes and aligns tiles according to the display's resolution).
